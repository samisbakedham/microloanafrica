Challenges for Traditional Microfinance

  • !High Default Rates: ~30.8% for MFIs in Sub-Saharan Africa, significantly higher than banks.
  • $Access to Capital: Low capital base and lower profitability (-2% ROA) hinder scaling.
  • ⚙️High Operational Costs: Range from 26% to 103% of loan portfolio, driven by manual processes and infrastructure challenges.
  • 📈Competition: Pressure from commercial banks and agile Fintechs.
  • ⚖️"Poverty Premium": High costs often lead to excessively high interest rates for the poorest borrowers.

Our AI-driven, digital-first model directly addresses these inefficiencies.

Key Unmet Credit Needs We Address

Many small businesses struggle with cash flow. Agri-SMEs in SSA face a ~$90B annual unmet loan demand. Our service can provide quick access to smaller loan amounts often overlooked by traditional lenders.

Individuals need funds for unexpected shocks (health, crop loss) and long-term goals (housing, education). Nigeria shows huge gaps for payday loans, rent, household goods, etc.

Women own ~40% of MSMEs in Nigeria but face disproportionate barriers. 56% of female entrepreneurs had loan applications rejected vs. 17% for men, often due to lack of collateral. Our alternative data approach can help bridge this gap.

Leveraging AI and Alternative Data

Our core innovation lies in using Artificial Intelligence and diverse alternative data sources to build inclusive credit scoring models. This allows us to assess the creditworthiness of the "invisible" population who lack formal credit histories, making financial services accessible to them.

The Power of AI in Credit Scoring

AI, especially machine learning, analyzes vast datasets beyond traditional financial records. It identifies complex patterns in alternative data (mobile usage, transactions, psychometrics) to predict repayment likelihood.

  • Creates nuanced credit profiles for the unbanked.
  • Can reduce default rates by up to 25% compared to traditional methods.
  • Facilitates personalized financial products.
  • Creates a "data network effect": more users -> more data -> better model -> better service.

Primary: Interest on Loans

Determined by: Cost of capital, operational costs, risk premium (minimized by AI), and a reasonable profit margin.

Secondary: Fee Structures

Navigating African Regulatory Terrain

Compliance is paramount. We will focus on countries with stable laws and proactively manage evolving regulations in data privacy, digital lending, and consumer protection.

Kenya Regulatory Snapshot

  • Data Privacy: Data Protection Act, 2019 (GDPR-aligned). ODPC Guidance for DCPs. Explicit consent for alt. data crucial.
  • Digital Lending: CBK licenses Non-Deposit-Taking Credit Providers. Focus on responsible pricing, transparency.
  • Sandbox: CMA offers a regulatory sandbox.

Nigeria Regulatory Snapshot

  • Data Privacy: NDPA 2023 (GDPR-inspired). Explicit consent for sensitive data and automated decisions. Data localization for some categories.
  • Digital Lending: CBN & FCCPC oversight. Guidelines on transparency, fair collection.
  • Sandbox: CBN offers a regulatory sandbox.

South Africa Regulatory Snapshot

  • Data Privacy: POPIA 2013 (GDPR-aligned). Consent must be voluntary, specific, informed. Cross-border rules apply.
  • Digital Lending: National Credit Act (NCA) is comprehensive. NCR licenses credit providers. Caps on rates/fees. Strong consumer protection (no reckless lending).
  • Sandbox: IFWG Innovation Hub includes sandbox.

Illustrative summaries. Detailed legal counsel per market is essential.

Tala

Operating in Kenya, the Philippines, Mexico, and India, Tala utilizes smartphone data, analyzing over 250 data points from a customer's mobile device—including app usage, transaction history, call logs, and other behavioral patterns—to generate a credit score within minutes. This has enabled Tala to serve over 6 million customers, disbursing more than $2.7 billion in microloans, typically ranging from $10 to $500. Notably, their repayment rates consistently exceed 90%, even when serving primarily first-time borrowers with no formal credit history. Tala's machine learning models continuously learn and improve with each lending cycle. A key factor in their success is significantly lower operational costs per loan, approximately 80% less than traditional MFIs.

Branch International

Active in Kenya, Nigeria, and Tanzania, Branch employs a similar AI-driven model. Their algorithm considers diverse factors such as mobile money transaction consistency, contact list diversity, and even seemingly unrelated data like battery charging habits to assess reliability and financial stability. Branch has disbursed over $600 million in loans to more than 3 million customers across its African markets.

Periculum

This Nigerian fintech startup leverages AI and alternative data to provide credit assessment solutions. Initially targeting large banks, Periculum pivoted to partner with more agile microfinance institutions and fintech startups across East and West Africa. A partnership with one leading MFI resulted in a 60% increase in loan processing speed and a notable reduction in default rates, empowering these institutions to extend credit to more underserved entrepreneurs. Periculum’s services include identity verification and fraud prevention alongside AI-driven credit risk assessment.
